Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • The novel begins in the midpoint of an unspecified wartime evacuation, where a British plane carrying boys crashes onto an uninhabited island.
  • While throwing rocks into the lagoon, a fair-haired boy meets a chubby boy wearing spectacles.
  • The fair-haired boy is named Ralph and the chubby boy is named Piggy.
  • Greatly intrigued by the whereabouts of the rest of the boys, Ralph and Piggy go looking for sign of life elsewhere.
  • During their search, the boys find a large conch shell. Piggy then suggests using the conch as a calling to the other boys.
  • The loud sound of Ralph's blow in the conch alerts the other boys, and they quickly gather around Ralph. The pilot remains unfound.
  • The boys' ages range between 6 and 12. Jack is among the oldest, and is also head of the boy's choir.
  • Piggy quickly becomes an outcast within the circle of boys, due to his physical appearance as well as his funny nickname.
  • The boys elect a leader, and Ralph wins the vote. Jack is noticeably angered by this election, as he wanted to be leader.
  • Ralph decides to go explore the island, bringing Jack and a choirboy named Simon with him.
  • During their exploration, the boys discover that they are the only humans living on the island.
  • On their way back to the beach, the boys stumble upon a wild pig tangled in vines. As Jack gets ready to kill the pig, he hesitates, and the pig runs away.
  • The boys continue through the jungle, making it back safely to the beach.
  • Chapter 2
  • As soon as he returns, Ralph demands an immediate meeting by sounding the conch shell.
  • During this first assembly, Ralph lays down certain rules. He announces to the boys that there are no adults on the island; therefore they will need to look after themselves.
  • In hopes to keep order, the boys need to lift up their hands. To speak, the chosen boy needs to hold the conch.
  • Piggy reminds the boys that they could be stuck on the island for a long time, seeing as no one knows they have crashed.
  • This thought spreads fear among the boys, as they quickly become silent.
  • A frightened young boy claims he has seen a snakelike monster. The mood suddenly changes as fear occupies the boys' imaginations. Ralph quickly assures them that there is no snakelike “beastie”.
  • To facilitate rescue, Ralph asks the boys to build a fire as an SOS signal.
  • With the help of the lens on Piggy's glasses, the boys get a fire going, but it slowly dies.
  • Piggy then whines about the boys' disorganization.
  • The choirboys are in charge of keeping the signal fire going.
  • Piggy is the outcast.
  • Chapter 3
  • Jack pursues a second hunt, but he is once again unsuccessful, as the pig runs away.
  • Back at the beach, Ralph and Simon are building huts. Beyond frustrated by the fact that no one is helping him out, Ralph notices that the boys are having fun playing in the lagoon.
  • A conflict arises between Jack and Ralph, due to their different priorities on the island. Ralph wants shelter and fire, whereas Jack wants meat.
  • Because he has failed twice, Jack is intent on hunting. He assures Ralph that he will kill the pig next time.
  • Simon is not slacking like the other boys. He has been secretly wandering off into the depths of the jungle to explore.
  • Chapter 4
  • A rhythm on the island is quickly established.
  • The activities of the youngest boys, also known as the "littluns", are very basic. They eat, cry for their mothers, obey to the conch and build sand castles.
  • Roger and Maurice are distinguished as the bullies on the island. They stomp on the "littluns"'s sand castles and throw rocks around them.
  • To prepare for the hunt, the hunters paint their faces with colored clay.
  • On the beach, Ralph and Piggy notice a ship. Aware that the signal fire has gone out, Ralph's fury with Jack increases since it is the hunter's jobs to keep the fire going.
  • The hunters return from the hunt chanting, "kill the pig", proudly carrying the dead animal on a stake.
  • Ralph communicates his anger towards the hunter's irresponsibility but the hunters are too proud to pay any attention to Ralph's speech.
  • Jack takes out his humiliation on Piggy. He punches him in the stomach and smacks his head, breaking a lens off his glasses.
  • The boys get the fire going and roast the pig.
  • Ralph heads down to the beach alone, as he is calling a meeting.
  • Chapter 5
  • Ralph's meeting is set to place some order on the island, since the boys have failed to follow the ground rules.
  • He adds emphasis on the importance of the signal fire, as well as the importance of building shelter.
  • Ralph reassures the littluns that there are no beasts on the island.
  • All the boys have different theories about the "beastie". Jack announces that if there is a beast, he will go hunt it down.
  • Jack and the hunter boys run off, leaving Ralph, Piggy and Simon behind.
  • Ralph announces to Piggy and Simon that he is considering stepping down from being the leader of the group. Piggy and Simon convince him that the boys all desperately need his leadership.
  • Chapter 6
  • Military airplanes are in battle above the island while the boys are sleeping.
  • A dead parachutist falls down from the sky. He lands on the rocks, with his chute tangled and flapping in the wind.
  • When the twins Sam and Eric wake up, they hurry to get the fire going.
  • The twins then notice the shadowy figure of the dead parachutist, immediately thinking that what they see is the beast.
  • After hearing the news, Ralph demands an immediate meeting. The boys are overcome with fear and dedication to find the beast and kill it.
  • Armed with wooden spoons, the boys leave the beach in hopes to find the beast.
  • The boys reach an unknown part of the island. It is a narrow walkway leading to a rocky area surrounded by small caves.
  • Ralph is the first to explore, but the other boys soon join him.
  • As the boys start to have fun playing games with rocks, Ralph reminds them of their mission.
  • They then return to their home base to rebuild the fire.
  • Chapter 7
  • On their way back towards the signal fire, Ralph looks out at the ocean. He is overcome with a hopeless feeling, doubting the occurrence and possibility of a future rescue.
  • Simon convinces Ralph that they will get back home soon.
  • On their way back, the boys track a boar, which leads to a chase. With a spear, Ralph hits the boar in its snout.
  • Even though the boar manages to escape, Ralph remains very proud of his accomplishment.
  • As the boys act out their hunt, they get carried away by almost killing Roger, whom was acting as the wild boar.
  • Late at night, Ralph, Jack and Roger decide to climb the mountain where they think the beast might be.
  • Peering down from the summit, the three boys see a terrifying shadowy figure making a strange sound in the wind.
  • Scared, the boys race down the mountain to warn the others.
  • Chapter 8
  • The next morning, Jack blows the conch to call an assembly. He is trying to prove to the boys that he would be a better leader then Ralph.
  • As the others disagree with Jack, he angrily storms off the beach.
  • Simon suggests to the group that they should go climb the mountain, but the others think he is insane for wanting to go there.
  • The boys decide to move the signal fire to the beach instead of the mountain.
  • A good portion of the group eventually goes off to join Jack.
  • Jack announces to his band of followers that he is the leader and that his group will have fun.
  • Jack and his followers go hunting a sow. This hunt is a lot more violent and savage in comparison to the other ones.
  • The hunters place the head of the sow on a stick.
  • Jack and his followers return to the beach, showing off their new kill.
  • Before Jack's arrival, Simon slips into the jungle to continue his independent explorations.
  • This specific exploration differs from the others, as Simon finds the head of the sow on a stick, swarming with flies.
  • The sight of the head hypnotizes him. It then speaks to Simon as "The Lord of the Flies", saying that he will never escape him.
  • Terrified by the head, Simon faints.
  • Chapter 9
  • After leaving "The Lord of the Flies", Simon goes to the mountain where he sees the dead parachutist. He runs down to free the tangled parachute from the rocks.
  • Simon runs back to the beach to prove to the boys that they were mistaken about the beast.
  • In the meantime, Ralph and Piggy decide to attend Jack's feast in hopes that they will regain control of the group.
  • Jack's tribe is violently dancing and chanting around the fire as a figure runs outs of the jungle. Unable to distinguish that the shadow is Simons', the boys attack the figure thinking it is the beast.
  • Simon is brutally killed by the boys.
  • Simon's corpse is thrown in the rushing waves of the ocean during a vicious storm.
  • The boys run away screaming in fear as the parachutist's body is pushed by the wind, and onto the beach.
  • Chapter 10
  • Piggy, Sam, Eric, and a handful of littluns are the only boys staying with Ralph. All the others have joined Jack's tribe.
  • Within his tribe, Jack rules over the boys. He is a negative leader and beats his followers.
  • Jack's tribe believes that Simon was the actual beast, and that the beast never truly dies.
  • Jack, Ralph and Maurice invade Ralph's camp to steal Piggy's glasses in order to make a fire.
  • Chapter 11
  • The next morning, after their failure to make fire without the glasses lens, Ralph and his companions decide to go pay a visit to Jack's tribe. Ralph plans to get Piggy's glasses back.
  • Upon their arrival, Jack's tribe demands them to leave.
  • Ralph demands Piggy's glasses.
  • Jack attacks Ralph, and then orders his hunters to tie up Sam and Eric.
  • Ralph is furious and fights Jack for the second time.
  • Piggy cries out, trying to bring the group back to sanity. Roger pushes a boulder down the mountainside, striking Piggy and the conch.
  • Piggy dies and Ralph runs away.
  • Chapter 12
  • Ralph is devastated by the deaths of Simon and Piggy. He realizes that evil has overcome the boys of the island.
  • Ralph finds the sow's head. He knocks it over to take the stake it was on. Ralph plans to use this weapon against Jack and his tribe.
  • After being tortured by Jack's tribe, Sam and Eric tell Ralph that Jack will be sending his tribe after him.
  • Ralph finds refuge in a thicket where he falls asleep.
  • Ralph smells smoke and realizes that Jack has set the jungle on fire.
  • The instant Ralph leaves his hiding spot; Jack's tribe swarms him.
  • Ralph franticly runs to find a safe place. He ends up face down on the beach, fighting to catch his breath.
  • He looks up to the horizon to find a naval officer.
  • The fire racing though the jungle caught the attention of the ship.
  • As Jack's tribe arrives on the beach, the officer assumes that the boys were playing a game.
  • When the officer is told otherwise, he is dumbfounded at how quickly these English boys have forgotten the rules of civilization.
  • Overwhelmed by their rescue, the boys begin to sob.
  • Embarrassed at the sight of the crying boys, the officer turns his back to the boys, giving them a chance to regain composure.
  • The novel ends with the sight of the officers' ship, involved in as much violence as the ferocity of the island.
